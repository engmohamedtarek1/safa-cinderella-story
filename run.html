Cinderella


Once upon a time there was a beautiful girl called Cinderella

and she had two ugly step sisters who were very unkind who made her do all the hard work.

She had to sweep the floors and do all the dishes while they dressed up in fine clothes and went to lots of parties.

1

One day

a special invitation arrived at Cinderella's house.

It was from the royal palace.

The king's only son, a truly handsome prince, was going to have a grand ball.

Three girls were invited to come.

Cinderella knew she wouldn't be allowed to go to the ball.

2

When the day of the ball came, they made such a fuss.

Poor Cinderella had to rush about upstairs and downstairs.

She fixed their hair in fancy waves and curls.

She helped them put on their expensive new dresses, and she arranged their jewels just so.

3

As soon as they had gone, Cinderella sat down by the fire and she said.

“Oh, I do wish I could go to the ball.”

The next moment, standing besid her was a lovely old lady with a silver wand in her hand.

“Cinderella,” she said I am your fairy godmother,

and you shall go to the ball. But first you must go into the garden and pick a golden pumpkin,

then bring me six white mice, a whiskered cat, and six butterflies.

You’ll find them in the garden.

4

So Cinderella fetched a golden pumpkin, six white mice, a whiskered cat, and six butterflies.

The fairy godmother touched them 12 with her wand and the pumpkin became a golden coach,

the white mice became six white horses,

the cat became a coachman with the most enormous moustache, and the butterflies became six footmen.

5

The fairy godmother touched Cinderella with the wand and her old dress became a golden dress sparkling with jewels while on her feet was the prettiest pair of glass shoes ever seen.

“Remember,” said the fairy godmother “you must leave the ball before the clock strikes twelve because at midnight the magic ends.”

6

When Cinderella arrived at the ball she looked so beautiful that everyone wondered who she was!

Even the ugly sisters.

The Prince asked her to dance with him and they danced all evening.

Now Cinderella was enjoying the ball so much that she forgot her fairy godmother’s warning.

7

It was almost midnight and the clock began to strike.

One.

Two.

Three.

She hurried out of the ballroom.

Four.

Five.

Six.

As she ran down the palace steps one of her glass shoes fell off.

Seven.

Eight.

Nine.

Cinderella ran on towards the golden coach.

8

The magic ends, Cinderella was in her old dress.

The golden coach became pumpkin, the six white mice and a whiskered cat ran away and the six butterflies flew.

So Cinderella had to walk home and by the time the ugly sisters returned home she was sitting quietly by the fire.

9

Now

when Cinderella ran from the palace, the prince tried to follow her and he found the glass shoe.

He said,

“I shall marry the beautiful girl whose foot fits this shoe and only her.”

In the morning the prince went from house to house with the glass shoe and every young lady tried to squeeze her foot into it.

But it didn’t fit any of them.

10

At last the prince came to Cinderella’s house.

First one ugly sister tried to squash her foot into the slipper.

But her foot was too wide and fat.

Then

the other ugly sister tried but her foot was too long and thin.

11

“Please,” said Cinderella,

“let me try.”

“The shoe won’t fit you”, said the ugly sisters.

“You didn’t go to the ball!”

But Cinderella slipped her foot into the glass shoe and it fit perfectly

The fairy godmother appeared again and touched Cinderella with the wand and there Cinderella was in a golden dress sparkling with jewels.

12

The Prince asked Cinderella to marry him.

There was a happy wedding.

Everyone who had gone to the ball was invited, even the ugly sisters.

There was wonderful food, lots of music and dancing.

And the Prince of course danced every dance with Cinderella.

He would not dance with anyone else.
